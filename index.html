Hypothesis & Findings

Hypothesis 1: The average resale price of HDB flats varies distinctly by flat type over the years.

Hypothesis 2: There is a polynomial relationship between floor area, remaining lease duration, flat type, and resale price. Specifically, we expect larger floor areas and longer remaining leases to positively correlate with higher resale prices across different flat types.

Findings: The interactive visualisation indicates clear patterns supporting the hypothesis. Average resale prices vary significantly by flat type, with larger units (e.g., Executive and Multi-Generation flats) generally commanding higher prices. Additionally, we observed a strong positive correlation between floor area and resale price. Flats with longer remaining lease durations also tend to fetch higher resale values.
